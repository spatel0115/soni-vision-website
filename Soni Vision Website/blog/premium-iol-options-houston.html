Premium IOLs

Premium IOL Options in Houston:
Which Lens Is Right for You?

RS
Dr. Ruhi Soni, MD
March 2025  ·  8 min read

When a patient comes to me with a cataract diagnosis, the conversation almost always reaches the same point: "So what happens after you take out the old lens?" That's exactly the right question — and the answer has changed dramatically over the past decade.

The intraocular lens (IOL) implanted during cataract surgery is not a detail. It is one of the most important decisions you will make for your long-term vision. A standard lens corrects one distance. A premium lens can correct multiple distances — and for many patients, eliminates the need for glasses entirely. The lens stays with you for life, so choosing wisely matters.

Here is how I explain the options to my patients in Houston and Cypress.

What Is a Premium IOL?

During cataract surgery, the clouded natural lens is removed and replaced with an artificial intraocular lens. Every patient gets an IOL — that's unavoidable. The question is which type.

A standard monofocal IOL corrects vision at one distance — typically far. Most patients with a standard lens still need reading glasses after surgery. Insurance (Medicare and most private plans) covers this option.

Premium IOLs go further. They're designed to reduce or eliminate dependence on glasses at multiple distances. They come with an out-of-pocket upgrade cost — typically $1,500 to $3,500 per eye above what insurance covers — but for active patients who want independence from glasses, the investment is almost always worth it.

"The IOL implanted today will be your lens for the rest of your life. It is worth taking the time to choose the right one."

The Four Premium IOL Options We Offer

1. Multifocal IOL — The Most Popular Choice

Multifocal IOLs use concentric rings of different focal powers to provide simultaneous clear vision at near, intermediate, and distance ranges. Think of them like a lens with multiple prescriptions built into a single optic.

The results are impressive. The majority of patients with multifocal IOLs achieve complete independence from glasses — they can read a menu, use a computer, drive, and play golf all without reaching for eyewear. It's genuinely life-changing for the right patient.

The trade-off: some patients notice mild halos or starburst effects around lights at night, particularly in the first few months after surgery. For most, these diminish significantly as the brain adapts. For patients who frequently drive at night in demanding conditions, we sometimes recommend a different option.

Best for: Patients with an active lifestyle who want complete glasses independence and are willing to adapt to minor visual adjustments initially.

2. Toric IOL — Precision for Astigmatism

If you have astigmatism — an irregular curvature of the cornea that causes blurred vision at all distances — a standard or multifocal IOL alone won't fully correct it. A toric IOL is specifically designed with built-in astigmatism correction.

Toric IOLs are available in both monofocal (distance-only) and multifocal versions. The key is precise surgical alignment — the lens must be oriented at exactly the right axis to correct your astigmatism. This is a technique-dependent procedure where surgical experience makes a real difference.

Best for: Any patient with significant astigmatism who wants the clearest possible uncorrected distance vision after surgery.

3. Extended Depth of Focus (EDOF) IOL

EDOF lenses take a different approach. Rather than multiple focal points, they create a single elongated focal zone that provides excellent distance and intermediate vision — computer screens, dashboards, faces across a room — with fewer halos than traditional multifocal lenses.

The trade-off is that most EDOF patients still need reading glasses for very fine print (small text, medication labels). But for patients who primarily want crisp distance and computer vision with excellent night driving performance, EDOF is often the ideal choice.

Best for: Frequent night drivers, patients with high-detail visual demands, or those who had concerns about multifocal halos.

4. Light-Adjustable Lens (LAL) — Maximum Precision

The Light-Adjustable Lens is the most technologically advanced IOL available today — and the only one that can be customized after it's been implanted. After surgery, a series of UV light treatments are used to fine-tune your prescription with extraordinary accuracy. Once the ideal correction is achieved, the lens is permanently locked in.

This is particularly valuable for patients who have had prior LASIK or PRK surgery, where standard IOL power calculations are less predictable. The ability to adjust after implantation provides a safety net that other IOLs cannot offer.

Best for: Patients who want the most precise possible outcome, especially those with a history of refractive surgery.

How We Decide Which IOL Is Right for You

The IOL recommendation is never one-size-fits-all. At Soni Vision Institute, we combine advanced biometry (measurements of your eye's length, curvature, and optical characteristics), a thorough lifestyle assessment, and an honest conversation about your priorities before making any recommendation.

  • What does a typical day look like for you — outdoor activities, computer work, driving at night?
  • What would bother you more: occasional reading glasses, or a brief adaptation period with night halos?
  • Do you have any corneal irregularities, dry eye, or history of refractive surgery that affects the options?
  • What are your expectations for the outcome, and are they realistic given your anatomy?

We will never recommend a premium IOL just to upsell you. And we will never recommend a standard lens just because it's easier. Our job is to match the right lens to the right patient — every time.

Are Premium IOLs Worth the Cost?

This is the question I hear most often. My answer: for most active patients, absolutely.

Consider what glasses and contacts cost over a lifetime — not just in dollars, but in inconvenience, limitation, and frustration. A premium IOL is a one-time investment in permanent vision correction. Most of our patients who choose premium lenses tell us it was the easiest decision they've ever made in hindsight.

That said, a premium IOL is not right for every patient. If you have certain corneal or retinal conditions, the benefit may be limited. We will always give you an honest assessment — not a sales pitch.
