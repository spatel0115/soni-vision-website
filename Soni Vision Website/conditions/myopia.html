Eye Conditions

Myopia
(Nearsightedness)

Myopia is the most common refractive error worldwide. Objects far away appear blurry while close objects remain clear. Surgery can provide lasting freedom from glasses and contacts.

Understanding the Condition

What Is
Myopia?

The eyeball is slightly too long, or the cornea is too steeply curved — causing light to focus in front of the retina instead of directly on it. The result is crisp near vision but blurry distance vision.

Myopia typically develops during childhood and adolescence and tends to stabilize in the early to mid-twenties. Once stable, a range of surgical procedures can permanently reduce or eliminate dependence on glasses and contact lenses. For high myopia that makes LASIK impractical, the EVO ICL offers an excellent lens-based alternative.

Signs to Watch For

Symptoms of
Myopia

Many people live with myopia for years before seeking a permanent solution. Recognizing the symptoms is the first step.

01

Blurry Distant Vision

Road signs, whiteboards, and faces across a room appear out of focus, while reading or looking at a phone is completely clear.

02

Squinting at Faraway Objects

Instinctively narrowing the eyes to partially compensate for the blur — a telltale habit of uncorrected myopia.

03

Eye Strain & Headaches

Extended effort to focus on distant objects leads to fatigue around the eyes and tension headaches, especially by end of day.

04

Poor Night Driving

Reduced contrast sensitivity in low light makes nighttime driving feel unsafe — halos and starbursts around headlights are common.

Treatment Options

How We
Treat Myopia

Once myopia has stabilized, several permanent correction options are available. The right choice depends on your prescription, corneal anatomy, and lifestyle preferences.

LASIK

The gold-standard laser procedure for most myopic patients. The excimer laser reshapes the corneal surface to redirect light precisely onto the retina. Most patients achieve 20/20 or better the day after surgery.

PRK

An excellent alternative to LASIK — especially for patients with thinner corneas or active lifestyles. The same laser technology, without a corneal flap.

EVO ICL (for High Myopia)

An implantable collamer lens placed inside the eye — ideal for patients with prescriptions too high for LASIK, or those who prefer to preserve corneal tissue. Reversible and removable.

Refractive Lens Exchange

For patients over 40 with high myopia, replacing the natural lens with a premium IOL corrects vision and eliminates the future need for cataract surgery.

Common Questions

Myopia
FAQ

What our patients most want to know before pursuing surgical correction.


Ask Us Directly
Can myopia be permanently corrected?

Yes — for the vast majority of patients with stable myopia, LASIK, PRK, or EVO ICL provides permanent correction that lasts decades. The procedures correct the shape of the eye itself (or add a permanent lens), so there is no ongoing dependency on external correction. Some patients may still need mild correction for specific tasks as they age, particularly as presbyopia develops after 40, but the dramatic reduction in dependence on glasses is lasting.

What level of myopia qualifies for LASIK?

LASIK is FDA-approved for myopia up to approximately -12.00 diopters, though most surgeons prefer to treat prescriptions up to about -8.00 to -9.00 diopters to preserve adequate corneal thickness. Beyond that range, EVO ICL is often the superior option. The final determination requires a comprehensive workup including corneal topography and pachymetry — measurements we take at your consultation.
