Eye Conditions

Presbyopia —
Age-Related Near Vision Loss

Presbyopia is the gradual loss of near vision that virtually everyone experiences after age 40. It occurs when the eye's natural lens loses flexibility. Several premium surgical options can restore reading vision.

Understanding the Condition

What Is
Presbyopia?

As the crystalline lens stiffens with age, it loses its ability to flex and focus on near objects — making reading glasses or bifocals necessary for most adults over 40 to 45. Unlike myopia or hyperopia, presbyopia is not a structural abnormality; it is a natural consequence of aging that affects nearly everyone.

The good news is that modern premium intraocular lenses — used in refractive lens exchange or cataract surgery — can restore a full, continuous range of vision. Many of our patients over 50 achieve complete independence from reading glasses through these procedures.

Signs to Watch For

Symptoms of
Presbyopia

Presbyopia typically develops gradually in your early to mid-forties. Here is what to look for.

01

Difficulty Reading Small Print

Menus, phone screens, and fine print that once were easy to read now appear blurry and require effort to decipher.

02

Holding Items at Arm's Length

Instinctively extending reading material further away to find the focal distance — a classic early sign of presbyopia.

03

Eye Strain with Near Work

Fatigue, burning, or aching around the eyes after reading, using a computer, or doing close detail work — particularly later in the day.

04

Need for Brighter Lighting

Tasks that were manageable in dim light now require good illumination — the aging lens transmits less light and accommodates less efficiently.

Treatment Options

How We
Treat Presbyopia

Premium IOL technology has transformed the treatment of presbyopia. Today's lens options can restore near, intermediate, and distance vision simultaneously — eliminating or dramatically reducing dependence on reading glasses.

Multifocal IOLs

Multiple focal zones within the lens allow simultaneous near, intermediate, and distance vision. Most patients achieve complete spectacle independence with excellent near vision.

EDOF Lenses

Extended depth-of-focus IOLs provide a continuous range of vision from intermediate to distance — with fewer visual side effects than traditional multifocal lenses. Ideal for patients who prioritize quality of vision at distance.

Refractive Lens Exchange

For patients who want freedom from reading glasses without a cataract diagnosis, RLE replaces the natural lens with a premium IOL — correcting presbyopia along with any existing refractive error.

Monovision LASIK

One eye is corrected for distance and the other for near, allowing the brain to blend both inputs. A well-established option for appropriate candidates who do well with monovision.

Common Questions

Presbyopia
FAQ

Clear answers to what our patients most want to understand about presbyopia and its treatment.


Ask Us Directly
Is presbyopia the same as farsightedness?

No — though both affect near vision, they have different causes. Hyperopia (farsightedness) is caused by the shape of the eye, and it is present from early life. Presbyopia is caused by the gradual stiffening of the eye's natural lens and develops with age, typically after 40, regardless of whether the person was previously nearsighted or farsighted. It is possible to have both conditions simultaneously.

Can presbyopia be surgically corrected?

Yes — refractive lens exchange with a premium IOL is the most effective way to address presbyopia surgically. By replacing the stiffened natural lens with a multifocal or EDOF IOL, patients can regain a broad range of clear vision without reading glasses. This procedure is essentially the same as cataract surgery and carries a similar safety profile. Many patients describe the result as better vision than they have had since their twenties.
