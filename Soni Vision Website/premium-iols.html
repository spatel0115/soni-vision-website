Understanding IOLs

Your Lens
for Life

During cataract surgery, the clouded natural lens is removed and replaced with an artificial intraocular lens (IOL). The IOL takes over the focusing job your natural lens once performed.

Standard insurance-covered IOLs correct vision at one distance — usually far. That means most patients still need reading glasses after surgery. Premium IOLs go further, offering clear vision at multiple distances and the potential to eliminate glasses entirely.

At Soni Vision Institute, we specialize in premium IOL selection and implantation. We take the time to match each patient to the lens that best fits their eyes, their lifestyle, and their vision goals.

Most Popular

Multifocal IOL

Uses concentric rings of different focal powers to provide clear vision at near, intermediate, and far distances — simultaneously. Most patients with multifocal IOLs achieve complete independence from glasses and contacts.

  • Clear vision at all distances
  • Most patients never need glasses again
  • Ideal for active lifestyles
  • May cause mild halos at night initially
Best for Astigmatism

Toric IOL

Designed specifically to correct astigmatism — an irregular curvature of the cornea — at the time of cataract surgery. Provides sharper distance vision for patients who would otherwise need astigmatism-correcting glasses after surgery.

  • Corrects astigmatism precisely
  • Superior distance vision
  • Available in monofocal and multifocal versions
  • Often combined with other premium IOL types
Smooth Transition

Extended Depth of Focus (EDOF)

Creates a single, elongated focal point rather than multiple separate zones. Provides excellent distance and intermediate vision with fewer halos and glare than traditional multifocal IOLs — ideal for patients who drive frequently at night.

  • Excellent distance & computer vision
  • Reduced nighttime halos vs. multifocal
  • May still need glasses for fine print
  • Excellent option for night drivers
Advanced

Light-Adjustable Lens (LAL)

The only IOL that can be customized after implantation using UV light treatments. After surgery, your prescription is fine-tuned to your exact vision — then locked in permanently. Ideal for patients who want the most precise possible outcome.

  • Post-surgical vision customization
  • Highest accuracy of any IOL available
  • Multiple light treatments after surgery
  • Exceptional for patients with prior LASIK

Ask Us Directly
How much do premium IOLs cost?

The cost of the premium IOL upgrade (above what insurance covers) typically ranges from $1,500 to $3,500 per eye depending on the lens type. We provide transparent, itemized pricing at your consultation — no surprises. We offer financing through Alphaeon Credit.

Will I really never need glasses again?

Most patients with multifocal IOLs achieve functional independence from glasses for the vast majority of daily activities. A small percentage still prefer reading glasses for very fine print in dim lighting. We'll set honest expectations during your consultation based on your specific anatomy and lifestyle.

What are the risks of premium IOLs?

Premium IOLs carry the same surgical risks as standard IOLs, which are very low. Some patients experience temporary halos or glare around lights at night with multifocal lenses, which typically diminish over 3–6 months as the brain adapts. We'll discuss all risks thoroughly before your procedure.

Can I choose a premium IOL if I've had LASIK?

Yes — though prior LASIK can affect the accuracy of IOL power calculations. We use specialized formulas and advanced biometry designed specifically for post-LASIK eyes. The Light-Adjustable Lens may be an especially good option in this situation, as it can be fine-tuned after implantation.
