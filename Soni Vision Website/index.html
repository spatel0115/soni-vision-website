Am I a candidate for cataract surgery?

If you're experiencing blurry vision, increased glare, halos around lights, or difficulty with night driving, you may have cataracts. Most patients are candidates once cataracts begin affecting daily activities. During your consultation, Dr. Soni or Dr. Reddy will perform a comprehensive evaluation and review all your options.

What is the difference between standard and premium IOLs?

Standard monofocal IOLs correct vision at one distance — usually far — meaning you'll still need reading glasses. Premium IOLs (multifocal, toric, extended depth-of-focus) are designed to provide clear vision at multiple distances and can often eliminate the need for glasses entirely after surgery.

How long is recovery after cataract surgery?

Most patients notice improved vision within 24–48 hours. Full recovery typically takes 2–4 weeks. You can usually resume light activities within a day or two, with follow-up appointments scheduled to monitor your progress.

Do you accept insurance?

Yes. We accept most major insurance plans for medically necessary cataract surgery. Premium IOL upgrades and elective refractive procedures (LASIK, EVO ICL) are typically not covered. Our team will review your coverage and provide transparent pricing before any procedure.

Is financing available for elective procedures?

Yes. We offer financing through Alphaeon Credit for LASIK, EVO ICL, and premium IOL upgrades. FSA and HSA funds can also typically be used for these procedures. Our team will walk you through all payment options at your consultation.
