Extended Depth of Focus lenses use advanced optical technology to elongate the range of clear vision — reducing dependence on glasses for most daily activities, with fewer visual disturbances than traditional multifocal lenses.

Understanding EDOF Technology

What Is an
EDOF Lens?

Standard monofocal IOLs focus at one fixed distance. Multifocal IOLs split light between multiple distinct focal points to cover near, intermediate, and distance — but this splitting can introduce halos and glare. Extended Depth of Focus lenses take a different approach: rather than creating multiple distinct focal points, they elongate the focal zone into a continuous range, providing a smooth, uninterrupted transition from distance through intermediate vision.

EDOF lenses such as the TECNIS Symfony and Vivity IOL achieve this through proprietary optical technologies — echelette diffraction, X-WAVE wavefront shaping, and others — that stretch the depth of focus without the abrupt focal point transitions of traditional multifocals. The result is excellent distance and intermediate vision with minimal optical side effects.

Continuous Vision Range

A smooth, uninterrupted range of clear vision from distance through intermediate — covering driving, computer work, and most daily tasks without glasses.

Reduced Halos vs. Multifocal

EDOF lenses produce significantly fewer and less intense halos and glare than traditional diffractive multifocal IOLs — an important advantage for night drivers.

Excellent Distance & Intermediate

Crisp distance vision for driving and outdoor activities, combined with clear intermediate vision for screens and face-to-face interactions.

Great for Active Patients

Ideal for patients with demanding visual needs — golfers, drivers, professionals, and anyone who wants to reduce glasses use without accepting the trade-offs of a full multifocal.

The Process

Your EDOF Lens Journey
Step by Step

Selecting the right premium IOL requires a thorough evaluation and careful planning. Here is how we guide you from consultation to excellent vision.

01

Candidate Evaluation

We assess your corneal health, pupil size, lifestyle, and visual demands to confirm that an EDOF lens is the optimal choice for your eye and goals. Not every premium lens suits every patient — we take the time to individualize this decision.

02

Lens Selection

Based on your measurements and preferences, we select the specific EDOF platform best suited to your eye. We also discuss whether a toric EDOF lens is appropriate if astigmatism is a factor.

03

Precise Implantation

Your EDOF lens is implanted during cataract surgery using our standard outpatient technique. The procedure typically takes under 20 minutes, and you return home the same day.

Vision Optimization

Most patients notice excellent distance and intermediate vision within the first few days. We follow up at one day, one week, one month, and three months post-surgery to ensure your vision is performing at its full potential.

Clear answers to the questions patients ask most about Extended Depth of Focus lenses.


Ask Us Directly
How is an EDOF lens different from a multifocal?

A multifocal IOL splits incoming light between two or three distinct focal points — typically near, intermediate, and distance — using a diffractive ring pattern. This gives excellent near vision but can produce more noticeable halos and glare, particularly at night. An EDOF lens instead stretches the focal zone into a continuous elongated range, providing a smooth transition from distance through intermediate with fewer optical side effects. EDOF lenses typically provide slightly less near vision than a full multifocal, but with a better quality of vision profile for many patients.

Will I still need reading glasses with an EDOF lens?

Most EDOF patients achieve comfortable distance and intermediate vision without glasses — covering driving, computer use, watching television, and most daily activities. Fine near tasks such as reading small print in dim lighting may still benefit from a pair of reading glasses. Many patients find they use reading glasses only occasionally, if at all, but we set realistic expectations at your consultation based on your specific visual demands.

Who is the best candidate for an EDOF lens?

EDOF lenses are an excellent choice for patients who want a significant reduction in glasses use — particularly for distance and intermediate tasks — while minimizing the risk of halos and glare associated with full multifocal IOLs. They are especially popular among patients who drive frequently at night, work in low-light environments, or have had previous refractive surgery. A thorough preoperative evaluation will confirm whether your cornea, pupil, and visual goals are well-matched to an EDOF platform.
