Pre & Post-Op
Instructions

Preparing properly for your procedure — and following recovery guidelines — is essential to achieving the best possible outcome. Please read all instructions carefully and contact our office with any questions.

Procedure Instructions

Cataract Surgery
Instructions

Please follow these instructions exactly. If you have questions about your specific medications or health conditions, call our office before your surgery date.

Before Surgery

Pre-Op Instructions

  • Do not eat or drink anything after midnight the night before surgery.
  • Continue all regular medications unless specifically instructed otherwise by our team.
  • Arrange a driver — you cannot drive yourself home after surgery.
  • Do not wear makeup, lotions, or perfume on the day of surgery.
  • Bring a complete list of all current medications, including supplements.
  • Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing. Avoid turtlenecks or anything that restricts head movement.

After Surgery

Post-Op Instructions

  • Use all prescribed eye drops exactly as directed — do not skip doses or stop early.
  • Do not rub or press on your eye for any reason.
  • Wear the provided eye shield while sleeping for the first week after surgery.
  • Avoid swimming and hot tubs for 4 weeks following surgery.
  • Resume light activities the day after surgery; avoid heavy lifting (over 20 lbs) for 2 weeks.
  • Attend all scheduled follow-up appointments: day 1, week 1, and month 1.
  • Contact us immediately if you experience sudden vision loss, severe pain, or increasing redness.

Procedure Instructions

LASIK
Instructions

LASIK preparation begins several days before your procedure. Contact lens wearers must stop wearing lenses in advance to ensure accurate corneal measurements.

Before Surgery

Pre-Op Instructions

  • Stop wearing soft contact lenses at least 5 days before your evaluation; rigid gas-permeable lenses must be removed 2–4 weeks in advance.
  • Do not wear makeup, eye cream, or perfume on your surgery day.
  • Arrange a driver for both surgery day and your day-after follow-up appointment.
  • Take the prescribed mild sedative as directed before arriving — you will not be able to drive after taking it.
  • Get a full night's rest the night before surgery.

After Surgery

Post-Op Instructions

  • Use all prescribed drops as directed: antibiotic, steroid, and lubricating artificial tears.
  • Wear the provided protective goggles while sleeping for the first week to prevent accidental rubbing.
  • Do not rub your eyes for at least one month following surgery.
  • Avoid swimming, hot tubs, and submerging your eyes for 2 weeks.
  • Vision may fluctuate and appear hazy for 2–4 weeks — this is a normal part of healing.
  • Attend your day-after, week-1, and month-1 follow-up visits without exception.

Procedure Instructions

EVO ICL
Instructions

The EVO ICL is a minimally invasive implant procedure for patients who are not candidates for LASIK or who prefer a lens-based correction. Recovery is typically rapid, but following these instructions carefully is essential.

Before Surgery

Pre-Op Instructions

  • Stop wearing contact lenses at least 1 week before the procedure.
  • Begin using the prescribed pre-operative eye drops starting 2 days before surgery, as instructed.
  • Do not eat or drink anything after midnight the night before the procedure.
  • Arrange a driver — you will not be able to drive yourself home.

After Surgery

Post-Op Instructions

  • Begin all prescribed eye drops immediately after surgery as directed.
  • Do not rub your eyes under any circumstances.
  • Avoid strenuous physical activity and heavy lifting for 1 week following surgery.
  • Avoid swimming, contact sports, and submerging your eyes for 2 weeks.
  • Attend all scheduled follow-up visits: day 1, week 1, month 1, and month 3.
