Glaucoma Laser

Lower Eye Pressure.
Without Drops.

Selective Laser Trabeculoplasty (SLT) is a gentle, in-office laser procedure that lowers intraocular pressure in glaucoma patients — often reducing or eliminating the need for daily eye drops. Safe, effective, and repeatable.

The Procedure

How SLT
Works

SLT uses short, low-energy laser pulses targeted at the trabecular meshwork — the eye's natural drainage system located at the junction of the iris and cornea. This gentle stimulation triggers a biological response that improves fluid outflow from the eye, reducing intraocular pressure without damaging surrounding tissue.

The procedure is performed at a slit lamp in our office and takes under 5 minutes. There are no incisions, no injections, and no recovery period. Most patients resume normal activities immediately. Pressure reduction typically develops over 4–8 weeks as the trabecular meshwork responds to treatment.

No Incisions Required

SLT is entirely non-invasive. The laser is delivered through a lens placed on the eye — no cutting, no stitches, no downtime.

In-Office Procedure (5 min)

Performed at the slit lamp in our clinic. You'll be home within the hour and can resume most normal activities the same day.

Reduces or Eliminates Eye Drops

Many patients achieve meaningful IOP reduction that allows them to reduce their drop burden — or eliminate drops entirely.

Can Be Repeated if Needed

Unlike some laser procedures, SLT can be safely repeated if IOP rises again over time — making it a durable, long-term management tool.

What to Expect

The SLT
Treatment Process

SLT is one of the most straightforward procedures in glaucoma care. Here is exactly what the process looks like from start to finish.

01

Anesthetic Eye Drops

Numbing drops are instilled in the eye. A special contact lens is placed on the eye surface to focus and direct the laser precisely to the trabecular meshwork. The procedure is painless.

02

SLT Laser Applied

Short pulses of low-energy laser light are applied to the drainage angle of the eye. The full treatment takes under 5 minutes and involves no cutting or tissue removal.

03

Pressure Monitoring (1–2 Weeks)

A follow-up appointment 1–2 weeks after treatment checks your IOP response. Pressure reduction develops gradually over 4–8 weeks as the trabecular meshwork responds to the laser stimulation.

04

Long-Term IOP Management

Based on your pressure response, your treatment plan is adjusted. Some patients reduce or stop drops entirely; others continue on a reduced regimen. SLT can be repeated as needed.

Common Questions

SLT
FAQ

The most common questions about glaucoma laser treatment — answered honestly.


Ask Us Directly
How effective is SLT?

SLT is effective in approximately 75–85% of treated patients, typically achieving an IOP reduction of 20–30%. Clinical trials — including the landmark LiGHT Trial — have demonstrated that SLT is as effective as first-line eye drop therapy for newly diagnosed open-angle glaucoma, and achieves better long-term IOP control in many patients. Results vary depending on the type and severity of glaucoma, the individual's drainage anatomy, and other factors your surgeon will discuss during consultation.

Is SLT a permanent solution?

SLT provides sustained IOP reduction for an average of 1–5 years, though the duration varies among patients. When pressure eventually rises again, SLT can be safely repeated in most cases — restoring the pressure-lowering effect. This repeatability is one of SLT's key advantages: it is not a one-time treatment but a long-term management tool that can be employed multiple times throughout a patient's course of glaucoma care.

Is the procedure painful?

SLT is well-tolerated by the vast majority of patients. Numbing drops are applied before the procedure, so there is no pain during treatment. Some patients notice a mild pressure sensation or see flashes of light as the laser is applied — both normal and temporary. Afterward, mild eye irritation or slight blurring may occur for a few hours. Serious side effects are rare. Most patients are surprised by how quick and comfortable the experience is.
