Premium IOLs

The Only Lens
Adjusted After Surgery.

The Light Adjustable Lens (LAL) by RxSight is the only intraocular lens that can be customized and optimized after implantation — using non-invasive UV light treatments that fine-tune your prescription once your eye has fully healed.

The Technology

What Is the
Light Adjustable Lens?

Every other intraocular lens is a fixed optic — once it is implanted, its prescription cannot be changed. The Light Adjustable Lens is fundamentally different. Made of a proprietary photosensitive silicone material, it can be reshaped after surgery by exposing it to precisely calibrated ultraviolet light treatments delivered in the clinic.

This means your final prescription is not locked in on the operating table — it is determined after your eye has healed. Once you and your surgeon are satisfied with your vision, a final lock-in treatment permanently sets the lens at its optimized power. No other IOL currently available offers this level of post-surgical control.

The LAL is FDA-approved and is particularly well-suited for patients who have had prior corneal refractive surgery (LASIK, PRK), where IOL power calculation can be less predictable, and for anyone who demands the highest possible refractive accuracy from their cataract surgery.

Post-Surgery Fine-Tuning

Your prescription is customized after your eye has fully healed — eliminating the guesswork that affects all other IOL platforms.

Personalized to Your Healed Eye

Healing changes the eye. The LAL accounts for those changes — adjusting to the eye you actually have after recovery, not the eye we measured before surgery.

Highest Accuracy of Any IOL

Clinical studies demonstrate that LAL patients achieve target refraction more frequently than patients receiving any other IOL type — including other premium lenses.

FDA-Approved Technology

The RxSight Light Adjustable Lens received FDA approval in 2017 and has since been implanted in hundreds of thousands of eyes worldwide with an exceptional safety record.

The Process

Your LAL Journey
Step by Step

The Light Adjustable Lens process spans several weeks — and that time is what makes it the most precise IOL available. Here is exactly what to expect.

01

LAL Implantation

The Light Adjustable Lens is implanted during cataract surgery using the same outpatient technique as any other IOL. The procedure takes under 20 minutes. You begin wearing UV-protective glasses immediately after surgery and wear them continuously until your lock-in treatment is complete.

02

Healing Period

Over the next two to four weeks, your eye heals and your vision stabilizes. This healing period is essential — it allows your surgeon to evaluate your true post-surgical refraction before making any adjustments to the lens.

03

UV Light Adjustment Sessions

Once healing is complete, you return to the clinic for non-invasive UV light adjustment treatments. Each session takes only a few minutes. The light reshapes the photosensitive lens material — reducing residual nearsightedness, farsightedness, or astigmatism. Multiple adjustments are possible until you and your surgeon are satisfied with the result.

04

Lock-In Treatment & Final Vision

Once your target vision is achieved and confirmed, a final lock-in UV treatment permanently sets the lens at its optimized prescription. After lock-in, the UV-protective glasses are no longer required. Your vision is now final — and precisely tailored to your healed eye.

Common Questions

Light Adjustable Lens
FAQ

Everything patients want to know about the LAL — answered clearly.


Ask Us Directly
How many adjustment treatments are needed?

Most patients require two to four light delivery treatment sessions before the lock-in. The number depends on how much residual refractive error is present after healing and how precisely we want to dial in the final prescription. Some straightforward cases may need only two sessions; others with more complex prescriptions or particular demands may require up to four. After each treatment, you return in a few days so we can assess the change before proceeding with the next adjustment or the lock-in.

How long does each UV light treatment take?

Each light delivery treatment session takes approximately 90 seconds to three minutes in the clinic. The process is completely non-invasive — you sit at the Light Delivery Device (LDD), look at a fixation target, and the calibrated UV light does the work. There is no pain, no anesthesia, and no recovery time needed. You can drive yourself to and from these appointments once the healing period is complete.

Is the Light Adjustable Lens right for me?

The LAL is an excellent choice for patients who place a very high value on refractive precision — particularly those with prior LASIK or PRK (where IOL calculations are inherently less predictable), those with unusual prescriptions, and anyone who wants the most accurate post-surgical refraction currently achievable. It is also well-suited for patients who are willing and able to attend the multiple post-operative adjustment visits and to wear UV-protective glasses between implantation and lock-in. If you are looking for the highest accuracy available in any IOL, the LAL may be the right choice for you.
